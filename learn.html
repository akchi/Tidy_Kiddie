Coronavirus

COVID-19 a recently declared pandemic causes respiratory infections.

People in remote communites are at a higher risk as they are mobile and tend to travel often. Follow the below prevention measures to stay safe and healthy.

Influenza

Have you got your flu shot this year ?

Influenza occurs as seasonal cases. In temperate zones such as Australia, the outbreaks tend to occur in th winter months. Ensure you are vaccinated before the flu season starts.

Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander people, young children unders 5 years of age and pregnent woman are at a higher risk of this infection.

Are you in a high risk zone ?

Pertussis

Pertussis is commonly known as whooping cough Pertussis is highly contagious in its early stages.

In Victoria, there is a clear seasonal pattern in the spring and summer months. Ensure your child maintains high immunisation levels and good nutrition.

Are you in a high risk zone ?

Campylobacter Infection

Campylobacter infection is commonly known as traveller's diarrhoea.

It affects all age groups although most commonly affects young adults and children under 5 years of age. In Victoria, foodborne and waterborne outbreaks occur and it is likely that many outbreaks might not be detected.

Are you in a high risk zone ?
