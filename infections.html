Frequently Asked Questions

Who provides immunisation service?
Imuunisation is provide by:
  1. Local councils
  2. General Practitioners and medical clinics
  3. Hospitals
  4. Specially qualified pharmacists

Other Services who can provide immunisation are:
  • Community health services
  • Aboriginal health services
  • Few maternal and Child Health centres
  • Few obstetric services for pregnant women
How much is the immunisation likely to cost?
For vaccines available on the immunisation program for:
  • New borns
  • Children up to 4 years
  • Young people in secondary school

is free at local councils. A consultation fee may apply if it is obtained from the general practitioners.
Why do all children need vaccines?

Vaccines protect children from infectious diseases. It also helps to protect the health of those living in their community.

Every year, around 2 - 3 million deaths are prevented because of vaccination.

Why do children get more vaccines now than they did when I was young?

Our ability to prevent diseases has evolved with technology. Vaccines for human papillomavirus (HPV), meningococcal disease, chickenpox are few vaccines that have resulted from advancement in technology

Do vaccines have side effects?

Yes, vaccines have side effects, but they are all minor and goes away within a few days. Serious reactions to vaccines are extremely rare.

Common side effects - redness, swelling and tenderness at the injection site.

Is it safe to give my child more than one vaccine at the same time?

Yes, research shows that giving children more than one vaccine at the same time is safe. These vaccines can be either combination vaccines or concomitant vaccines

Stay Safe. Stay Healthy.
